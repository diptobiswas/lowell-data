üè≠ Birthplace of American Industry

First Planned Industrial City in America

In the 1820s, Francis Cabot Lowell studied British textile mills and built the first water-powered cotton mill in America. The city that bears his name became the "Cradle of the Industrial Revolution".

5.6 miles of canals powered dozens of mills. At its peak, Lowell produced more textiles than anywhere else in America.

Today, the Lowell National Historical Park preserves this legacy. The old mills? Many are now loft apartments renting for $1,848+/month.
